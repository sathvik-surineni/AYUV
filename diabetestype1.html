Type-1 Diabetes

This type is an autoimmune disease. In this case, the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas are destroyed. It’s usually diagnosed in children and young adults (but can develop at any age). It was known as “juvenile” diabetes. People with Type 1 diabetes need to take insulin every day. This is why it is also called insulin-dependent diabetes

In View Of Ayurveda

  • Ayurveda is an ancient system of medicine that originated in India and has been practiced for thousands of years.

  • It focuses on balancing the body's energies, or doshas, to promote health and well-being. While Ayurveda may have some insights into managing diabetes type 1, it's important to note that it does not provide a cure for the condition.

  • Diabetes type 1 is a chronic autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks and destroys the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas, resulting in a lack of insulin production.

Symptoms

  • Increased thirst.

  • Weak,tired feeling.

  • Blurred vision.

  • Numbness or tingling in the hands or feet.

  • Slow-healing sores or cuts.

  • Unexplained weight loss.

  • Frequent urination.

  • Dry mouth.

Causes of Type 1 Diabetes

  • Type 1 diabetes is a chronic autoimmune condition in which the body’s immune system attacks the insulin-producing cells inside the pancreas.

  • As a result, the body is no longer able to make insulin. Insulin is responsible for lowering blood glucose levels when they get too high.

  • Have an immediate family member with type 1 diabetes

  • Have certain autoantibodies in their blood.

  • Had certain viral infections.

  • Had certain environmental triggers.
