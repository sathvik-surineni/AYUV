Jaundice

Jaundice is also known as Icterus. It may happen at any age including infants, and adults. In dealing with jaundice, the skin turns to yellowish color, and the white part of the eye is also turned yellowish. It is due to the results of increased bile pigments and bilirubin in the blood.

In View Of Ayurveda

In Ayurveda, Jaundice is known as "Kamala". As Ayurveda says, our body is preserved by three essential energies known as vata, pitta, and kapha. Ayurveda classifies Kamala as an apratyaksha(indirect) disease. Disorder in any of the three energies results in a problem in the body. Aggravation of pitta reasons is a liver disturbance. Ayurveda considers the malformation of Ranjak pitta(a subtype of pitta) as amenable for jaundice.

Symptoms

  • Yellow skin and the white part of the eyes

  • Yellow color inside the mouth

  • Dark or brown-colored urine

  • Pale or clay-colored stools

  • Itching (pruritis) usually occurs with jaundice
