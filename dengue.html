Ayurvedic herbs

Giloy

  • Giloy is known for its immunomodulatory effects, meaning it can help regulate and strengthen the immune system. It may help the body defend against various infections and illnesses.

  • Giloy contains antioxidants that can help combat oxidative stress in the body. This may reduce the risk of chronic diseases and support overall health.

  • Some individuals may be allergic to Giloy or its components. Allergic reactions can range from mild skin rashes to severe allergic responses. If you experience any signs of an allergic reaction, such as itching, swelling, or difficulty breathing, discontinue use and seek immediate medical attention.

  • Pregnant and breastfeeding women should use Giloy with caution. There is limited research on its safety in these populations, so it's best to consult a healthcare provider before using it.

Mulethi

  • Mulethi, also known as Licorice or Glycyrrhiza glabra, is a widely used herb in traditional medicine systems, such as Ayurveda, Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), and Unani medicine. It offers various potential health benefits

  • Mulethi is known for its soothing properties and is often used to alleviate sore throat symptoms. It can help reduce throat irritation and coughing.

  • While Mulethi (Licorice or Glycyrrhiza glabra) offers various potential health benefits, it's important to be aware of the potential disadvantages and side effects associated with its use. These disadvantages can occur when Mulethi is consumed in excessive amounts or used improperly.

  • One of the most well-known disadvantages of Mulethi is its potential to raise blood pressure. It contains a compound called glycyrrhizin, which can lead to sodium and water retention in the body, ultimately causing an increase in blood pressure. Prolonged or excessive consumption of glycyrrhizin-rich products, such as Mulethi, can lead to hypertension.

Neem

  • Neem, scientifically known as Azadirachta indica, is a versatile and valuable tree native to the Indian subcontinent. Various parts of the neem tree, including its leaves, seeds, bark, and oil, have been used for centuries in traditional Ayurvedic and herbal medicine due to their numerous potential health benefits.

  • Antibacterial and Antifungal Properties: Neem contains compounds like azadirachtin and nimbin that have potent antibacterial and antifungal properties. Neem extracts are often used to treat various skin conditions and fungal infections.

  • Neem oil or neem-based products can sometimes cause skin irritation, itching, or redness in some individuals. Before applying neem topically, it's advisable to perform a patch test on a small area of skin to check for any adverse reactions.

  • Some people may be allergic to neem. Allergic reactions can range from mild skin irritation to more severe symptoms such as swelling, hives, or difficulty breathing. If you experience any signs of an allergic reaction, discontinue use and seek medical attention.

  • Datura, also known as jimsonweed or devil's trumpet, is a highly toxic plant that contains tropane alkaloids, including scopolamine, atropine, and hyoscyamine. Its consumption or use can have severe and potentially life-threatening disadvantages and side effects.

Punarnava

  • Punarnava, scientifically known as Boerhavia diffusa, is an herb that has been used for centuries in traditional Ayurvedic and herbal medicine systems. It is believed to offer various potential health benefits.

  • Punarnava is primarily known for its diuretic properties, which means it promotes urine production and helps the body eliminate excess water and toxins. This can be beneficial for individuals with edema (fluid retention), kidney issues, or high blood pressure.

  • Punarnava is often used to support kidney health. It may help in the management of conditions like kidney stones, urinary tract infections (UTIs), and kidney dysfunction.

  • In some cases, Punarnava supplementation may lead to mild digestive side effects, such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or stomach discomfort. If you experience gastrointestinal issues, discontinue use and consult a healthcare provider.
